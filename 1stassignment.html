Overview

Smoked meat is the result of the method of preparing meat and seafood that originated all the way back in the Paleolithic Era. This method provides flavor to the meat as well as change the appearance through the Maillard reaction which results in the iconic smoke ring present in smoked meats. Generally, there is two types of smoking, cold and hot with cold smoking occurring below 90 °F and hot smoking occurring above 160 °F. We will mainly focus on hot smoking with information on what temperature meat should be cooked to as well as the different types of wood available and popular types of smokers on the market.
