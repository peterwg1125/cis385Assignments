Overview

Before taking the first step to becoming a pro at smoking meat is to know exactly what temperatures are ideal for smoking different types of meat ranging from beef, pork, poultry, fish, and more. A good digital thermometer is vital to producing perfectly smoked meats that are both tender and flavorful. Below is a basic chart that provides general temperatures and estimated cook times for the above mentioned cuts of meat. Anyone can use this when smoking meat either for the first time, or the 1000th time.

Temperature Table

Meat Type Recommended Smoking Temp. Recommended Target Internal Temp. Estimated Time
Beef Brisket 225-250°F 195-207°F 12-20 hrs
Beef Short Ribs 225-250°F 200-210°F 6-8 hrs
Pork Ribs 225-250°F 190-205°F 5-7 hrs
Pork Butt 225-250°F 200-207°F 1.5 hr/lb
Whole Chicken 275-300°F 165°F 2-3 hrs
Salmon 200°F 145°F Starts to flake
Trout 225°F 145°F 1 hr
